# Decision Log

## Decision: Prioritise Hypotheses 1, 2, and 4 for the Week 3 technical slice

**Date:** Week 1

Reviewed all 5 hypotheses for technical feasibility. Hypotheses 1 (guided checklist), 2 (progress visibility), and 4 (contextual support) need no backend and can be built as small, self-contained components. Hypothesis 3 (centralised information hub) needs a real content/data structure and is better represented in Figma only. Hypothesis 5 (flexible onboarding) is a stretch goal if time allows after the core slice is working.

**Evidence informing this:** Feasibility review against handbook section 39 ("keep it small and focused") and each hypothesis's technical requirements.

**Alternatives considered:** Building the full onboarding hub (H3) as the technical demo — rejected as too broad for a Week 3 timeframe.

---

## Decision: Build a single combined "Day 1 view" rather than isolated components

**Date:** Week 3

Rather than building H1, H2, and H4 as three disconnected components, combined them into one focused screen (a Day 1 onboarding view) so the technical slice reads as a coherent product moment rather than three unrelated widgets, while keeping total scope small per handbook guidance.

**Evidence informing this:** Journey map Day 1 / First Week moments identified checklist + progress + help as tightly linked in the user's actual experience.

**Alternatives considered:** Building H1 alone as a single isolated card — rejected because it wouldn't demonstrate how progress and help support the checklist experience.

---

## Decision: Re-grounded hypotheses in real interview evidence

**Date:** Week 1 (post-interview collection)

Original hypotheses were placeholder examples. Once all 6 group transcripts (P01, P02, P03, P05, P06, P07) were available, rewrote all hypotheses to cite specific participant evidence and re-ran the feasibility pass. This surfaced one new hypothesis not in the original draft: H6 (transparent, source-cited AI support with clear human-handoff limits), which came up independently in all 6 interviews and was too consistent to leave out.

**Evidence informing this:** `research-synthesis.md`, 8 pain-point themes across 6 interviews.

**Alternatives considered:** Keeping the original 5 example hypotheses — rejected because they weren't traceable to actual research evidence, which the handbook requires (section 57, rule 8: "design decisions must connect back to research evidence").
