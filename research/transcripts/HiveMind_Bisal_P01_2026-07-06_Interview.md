# Section B — Work and Recent Onboarding

**[02:10] Interviewer:** Thinking back to when you first joined the company, what was your first week like?

**[02:16] Participant P01:** Honestly, it was quite overwhelming. Everyone was friendly, but there was just so much information. I spent most of my first day filling in forms, setting up passwords, collecting equipment, and watching mandatory training videos. When I actually got onto the shop floor, I wasn't really sure what I should focus on first.

**[02:55] Interviewer:** What made it feel overwhelming?

**[02:59] Participant P01:** There wasn't much structure after the basic induction. Different people showed me different things. One colleague explained the till system, another talked about customer returns, someone else mentioned stock checks, and by the end of the day I couldn't remember half of it.

**[03:40] Interviewer:** In the first two weeks, what were the top things you needed to know to do the job well?

**[03:45] Participant P01:** Definitely the till and returns system first, since that's what customers ask about most. Second, the escalation process—who to call if a customer became aggressive or if a refund required manager approval. Third, the shift rota system because I didn't know how to check my own hours during the first few days.

**[04:30] Interviewer:** Did onboarding feel helpful and relevant, or did it feel like box-ticking?

**[04:35] Participant P01:** A mix, really. The compliance training like health and safety and fire safety felt like box-ticking. The things that actually helped me do my job came from colleagues taking time out of their day to show me what to do.

# Section C — Finding Information at Work

**[05:10] Interviewer:** When you need an answer at work now, where do you go first?

**[05:14] Participant P01:** Usually I ask in our WhatsApp group with the other supervisors because it's quicker than searching the intranet.

**[05:35] Interviewer:** How often do you have to check more than one place to find what you need?

**[05:39] Participant P01:** Quite often, probably a few times each week. If nobody answers on WhatsApp, I'll search the intranet, and if that's outdated I'll email HR directly.

**[06:15] Interviewer:** Tell me about a time you couldn't find something important. What happened?

**[06:20] Participant P01:** Once I needed the updated returns policy during a busy period, but I could only find last year's version on the intranet. I gave a customer incorrect information about the refund window and later had to call them back to correct it.

**[07:00] Interviewer:** How do you personally keep track of important links, documents, or tasks?

**[07:04] Participant P01:** I keep a notebook. I've tried using the shared drive, but the folder structure changes so often that I stopped bookmarking anything.

**[07:40] Interviewer:** If you had one home screen at work showing the most important things, what would you want it to show?

**[07:45] Participant P01:** My team's rota for the week, any escalated customer cases that are still open, and a list showing who's on duty in each department.

**[08:20] Interviewer:** What information should be available within just a few clicks?

**[08:24] Participant P01:** The current returns and refunds policy. That changes regularly and causes the most confusion when people use an outdated version.

**[08:50] Interviewer:** When you find a document or answer, how do you know it's correct or up to date?

**[08:54] Participant P01:** Honestly, I don't always know. I usually trust whoever sent it to me, although I know that's not ideal.

# Section D — Starting a Job / Smart Onboarding

**[09:20] Interviewer:** On your first day, what would have made you feel less lost?

**[09:24] Participant P01:** A simple day-by-day plan. Something like Monday for tills, Tuesday for customer returns, Wednesday for inventory. Instead I was expected to learn naturally by asking whoever happened to be nearby.

**[10:05] Interviewer:** In your first week, did you clearly know what to do next each day?

**[10:09] Participant P01:** No. I'd finish one task and then wait for someone to tell me what to do next.

**[10:35] Interviewer:** What makes onboarding feel personal and useful rather than generic?

**[10:39] Participant P01:** It should focus on my actual role rather than only company-wide policies. Having regular manager check-ins would also make a big difference.

**[11:15] Interviewer:** What made you feel like you were doing well?

**[11:19] Participant P01:** My manager told me after a few weeks that I was doing a good job. Before that, I wasn't really sure.

**[11:50] Interviewer:** When you needed help, did you know who to ask?

**[11:54] Participant P01:** Not initially. It took around a month before I understood who specialised in different areas.

# Section E — AI Helper Trust

**[12:20] Interviewer:** Have you used AI tools at work, like chatbots or Copilot-style tools?

**[12:24] Participant P01:** A little. We trialled an AI chatbot for HR questions. It worked well for simple questions like holiday allowance, but it once gave outdated information about maternity leave.

**[13:00] Interviewer:** What would make you trust an AI tool's answer?

**[13:04] Participant P01:** Showing exactly where the information came from and when it was last updated.

**[13:30] Interviewer:** What would make you not trust it?

**[13:33] Participant P01:** If it gave confident answers without showing any supporting source, especially for company policies.

**[13:55] Interviewer:** What questions should AI avoid answering?

**[13:59] Participant P01:** Disciplinary issues, salary questions, grievances, or anything involving sensitive employee matters. Those should always go to a real person.

**[14:30] Interviewer:** Would you worry about privacy if AI used internal company documents?

**[14:34] Participant P01:** Yes. I'd want to know exactly what information it has access to, especially employee performance data.

**[15:34] Participant P01:** Mostly time. The incorrect refund information also reduced a customer's confidence in our service.

**[16:04] Participant P01:** A structured day-by-day onboarding plan designed specifically for the role instead of a generic company induction.

## Summary

### Key Pain Points

- No structured onboarding plan during the first week.
- Information stored across multiple systems.
- Difficulty identifying the latest version of company policies.
- Uncertainty about who to contact for help.
- Generic onboarding instead of role-specific guidance.

### User Needs

- A personalised onboarding journey.
- Centralised access to company documents.
- Clear document version control.
- Role-specific learning materials.
- Contact directory for key staff.
- Progress tracking and manager check-ins.
- AI answers linked to verified company sources.
